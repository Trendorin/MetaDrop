# Code of Conduct

MetaDrop contributors must keep project spaces professional, factual, and safe for people of different backgrounds and experience levels.

Acceptable participation includes constructive technical criticism, clear reproduction steps, respect for privacy, and willingness to correct mistakes. Harassment, threats, discriminatory language, publication of private information, deliberately unsafe samples, and repeated disruption are not accepted.

Maintainers may edit or remove comments, reject contributions, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban accounts when necessary to protect the project and its participants. Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately when possible, and avoid exposing a reporter or affected person.

Report conduct concerns privately through the maintainer contact options on the [GitHub profile](https://github.com/Trendorin). Security vulnerabilities must use the separate process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).
